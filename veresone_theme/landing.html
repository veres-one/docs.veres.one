Decentralization and cryptography increase cost-sharing and resiliency in the face of failure. The optimized fit-for-purpose architecture of the Veres One Blockchain results in decentralized identifier acquisition and management costs that are a fraction of popular ledgers like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

Unlike many other blockchain networks, Veres One does not use a scarce network token. This removes price volatility as well as regulatory risks associated with speculative token networks. With Veres One, you can expect low stable fees that decrease as the network scales.

There is no central authority. Participants rely on consensus to make writes to the ledger, with each preserving their own authority. The distributed nature of the blockchain is well suited for meeting the challenge of bringing disparate parties together around globally interoperable infrastructure for identity management.

Blockchains provide high-integrity, robust, and dependable data, even when accessed offline. Strong cryptography makes Veres One extremely resistant to attacks. Data tampering is detected and data integrity is preserved.

User-centric architecture minimizes the amount of data shared with third parties. People can choose which credentials, or even specific attributes, are shared with whom. Veres One is well suited to address the EUâ€™s new General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Veres One supports the entire spectrum of autonomy required for various identity management use cases. To this end, Veres One enables any person or organization to create and control their identifiers, aggregate data about themselves, and determine where that data is stored and who has access.

Global Public Utility

Veres One is a global public utility for individuals, enterprises, and governments.

Individuals

Veres One is designed to put people in control of their own identity information. Individuals can aggregate digital credentials about themselves from various parties and share them with whom they see fit. Veres One gives people the independence to freely change services without losing their online identity in the process.

Enterprises

Enterprises benefit from a cost-effective identity utility that is interoperable with anyone who uses Veres One blockchain. Authorized employees can seamlessly access restricted data without the need for usernames and passwords. Organizations gain the ability to create dynamic identity management rules with ease.

Governments

Whether it be national governments, smart cities, or other municipalities, Veres One provides interoperable identity management infrastructure that enables citizens to easily connect with a variety of separate government services using the same digital credentials.

Sectors

The Veres One Blockchain can be used across the entire spectrum of identity management use cases.

Government

Governments can issue digital credentials such as driverâ€™s licenses, passports, metro passes, food stamp vouchers, or voter IDs. Credentials are issued directly to citizens, enabling them to decide with whom the credentials are shared.

Healthcare

Veres One enables disparate entities across the healthcare system to exchange verifiable identity data. Physician licenses, digital prescriptions, and medical records are just a few examples of identity data that can be interoperably shared and verified.

Financial Services

Banks, credit unions, and other financial institutions benefit from stronger identity verification. Cost-effective, know-your-customer checks reduce fraud and ease regulatory compliance.

Underserved

Over one billion people worldwide are unable to prove their identity. This restricts their access to financial services, education, and healthcare. Providing the underserved with Veres One identifiers helps service organizations coordinate aid.

Trade

Every trade entity in a complex economy can be more strongly authenticated, reducing fraud, friction, and transit time across supply chains.

Social

Decentralized social networks, peer-to-peer messaging apps, and sharing economy apps can benefit from Veres One features such as user centricity and data portability.

Become an Accelerator

Accelerators can write to the Veres One Blockchain in a matter of seconds.

To create new services or integrate products with the Veres One Blockchain, write to the blockchain through an existing Accelerator or become an Accelerator.

Accelerators include registrars, governments, credential issuers, and digital wallet providers.

Anyone who accepts the Simple Accelerator Agreement may become an Accelerator.

Run a Node

Running a node of the Veres One Blockchain provides instant access to data and onsite backups.

A large number of nodes run by a variety of entities is essential for the health and security of the network. Nodes are financially compensated by the Veres One Project for their contribution to the network.

As the system is permissionless, any person or organization that follows the guidelines in the Simple Node Agreement may run one or more nodes.

How it works

It takes about three minutes to get a digital wallet and a Veres One identifier.

Issuers issue credentials to Veres One identifiers and wallets under the wallet ownerâ€™s control. The Wallet owner can then present those credentials to verifiers without the need for issuer notification.

Wallet owners gather credentials issued from various third parties and store them in digital wallets. Wallet owners share credentials with verifiers they choose in order to achieve their objective.

Wallet owners store their digital credentials in the cloud or on their devices for future use.
